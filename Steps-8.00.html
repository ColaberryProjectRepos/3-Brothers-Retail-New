Suggestions/Recommendations

1. Investigating shrinks in GPM.
To address the issue of the declining profit margin, it is necessary to conduct a thorough investigation into the underlying causes. It is important to understand why our profits are not scaling proportionately with the increase in sales. One potential factor could be a rise in supply or vendor costs. If this is identified as the root cause, we should evaluate the feasibility of adjusting our retail prices to offset these additional expenses. This analysis will enable us to make informed decisions and implement strategic adjustments to maintain our profitability, as we have experienced a reduction of 10% or more in profit margin in each category over the past year.

2. Cutting Costs.
In the interim, we should explore various cost-cutting opportunities across different facets of our operations. Reducing costs can improve our profit margins. Potential areas for cost reduction include production, operations, shipping, and warehousing.

3. Geographic Analysis.
Given that the majority of our sales are concentrated in three main regions, we need to consider this in our planning. This is an appropriate time to evaluate our distribution centers and determine if they are situated in optimal locations based on our sales. We need to consider both the operational costs of a location and its centrality to the regions they will serve. If they are currently not meeting our needs, relocating these centers could lead to future cost savings.
